# STAT 418: Computational Methods in Data Science — Syllabus (Demo)

This is a small synthetic syllabus used by `01_builtin_rag.py` to demonstrate
retrieval-augmented generation. It is not the real course syllabus.

## Prerequisites

Students must have completed STAT 301 (Introduction to Statistical Methods) or an
equivalent course, and CS 159 (Programming Fundamentals) or equivalent.
Familiarity with Python is expected. Students should be comfortable with basic
probability, hypothesis testing, and writing code for data analysis.

## Late Homework Policy

Homework submitted within 24 hours of the deadline receives a 20% penalty.
Homework submitted more than 24 hours late is not accepted without prior
arrangement with the instructor.

## Grading

Grades are based on homework (40%), two midterm exams (30%), and a final project
(30%). A grade of C or better is required to satisfy the program requirement.

## AI Policy

Use of AI assistants is permitted on homework provided that submissions are the
student's own work and any AI use is disclosed, consistent with Purdue's AI
Working Competency Requirement.
